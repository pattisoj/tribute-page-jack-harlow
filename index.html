1. Jack Harlow's Early Career.

Jackman Thomas Harlow was born on March 13th 1988 in Louisville Kentucky, USA. In his early life he always had a taste for rapping as he first began making songs with his friends around age 12. They sold copies of their first CD Rippin' and Rappin' at their highschool. In 2015 Jack Harlow releaed his first commercial album The Handsom Collection. This was followed by a release in 2016 named 18. Following these two album releases Harlow would go on to release two more singles in 2017 (Routine and Dark Knight). Dark Knight became the lead single on his album release of 2017 Gazebo, to commemorate the release of the album Harlow went on a 14-city Gazebo Tour.

2. From Chick-fil-A to Whats Poppin.

Jack moved to Atlanta in 2018 and worked at a Chick-fil-A while producing more music. After being signed to a record label Harlow released Loose as his major label debut album. The album was nominated for the best mixtape award at the 2019 BET Hip Hop Awards. 2019 also saw the release of Thru the Night which featured fellow Louisville native Bryson Tiller. This was followed by the 12 song album release of Confetti. On January 21, 2020 Harlow released the song that changed his life for good. Whats Poppin. Whats Poppin would chart at number 2 on the USA Billboard HOT 100 after its huge popularity on TikTok and youtube where it currently sits at 135million views. This would open many avenues for Harlow's future career.

3. The present and future of Jack Harlow.

Following the succes of Whats Poppin Harlow would release Sweet Action his second EP. In April 2020 Harlow and G-Eazy would collaborate on Moana. In June the remix of Whats Poppin was released, it featured DaBaby, Tory Lanez, and Lil Wayne. On August 11, Harlow was included in XXL's 2020 Freshman Class. This was followed with the release of Tyler Herro on October 22, 2020. This was the lead single from his debut studio album, That's What They All Say (released December 2021). In May 2021 Harlow would feature on the remix of Killer with Eminem and Cordae. He would also collaborate with Lil Nas X on Industry Baby the song reached number 1 on the Hot 100, giving Harlow his first chart topper. In 2022 Harlow released Nail Tech his first release in six and a half months, it debuted 18 on the Hot 100 and recieved praise from Kanye West. This lead to Harlow's feature on DONDA 2 with Louie Bags. On the 16th March 2022 Harlow announced his next album Come Home the Kids Miss You with a May 6th release date.
